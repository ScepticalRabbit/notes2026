1. Aptitude for learning technical skills - *Lloyd* 
"Tell me about the most difficult technical concept, software, or tool you had to teach yourself for your degree or a personal project. Walk me through your exact process for learning it."
- Looked into imaging methods for the interview / DIC. Started from first principles - why and then go from there. How does the method work and then go on to paper. Branching out starting from a core concept and moving on from there. Until get to bigger picture. 

2. Organisational skills - *Adel*
"Tell me about a time when a project you were organising started to go completely off track due to an unforeseen technical issues. How did you get things back on track?"

- 3rd year uni, processing lab. Recieve 3 magnesium samples and use these to get optimal processing parameters for these alloys. Had gant charts or organisational flow. One month in the cold rolling machine died and the dept only has one. Half the microcopes in the lab also failed. Also looking at heat treatment so completely focused on that first. Able to pivot and prioritise needs based on availability

3. Teamwork skills - *Joel* 
"In our team, we have a strong culture of peer review—whether that’s reviewing an experimental design, checking a simulation mesh, or proofreading a paper before we publish. How do you handle receiving critical, detailed feedback from a peer on something you’ve poured days of hard work into?"

- More critical feedback is better. Want to know where they have gone wrong. Good example with boxing. Criticism from masters project always focuses on improvements never on anything good.
4. Ability to work independently *Lloyd*
"Research often means hitting dead ends where the 'right' answer isn't an AI prompt away. Tell me about a time you were completely stuck on a problem and your supervisor was unavailable. What did you do?"

- Gave example of comsol simulation - simulation breaks for unkown reason. Got star patterns in simulation. Supervisor couldn't help because not computational. Do research on own and identify source of problem. COMSOL reddit and other forums. Experiment to isolate the problem. 

5. Research motivation - *Adel*
"What specifically draws you to a non-commercial, research-focused environment like our team at UKAEA?"

- Likes deep tech like fusion, very exciting frontier. Many complex problems to sink teeth into. Really believes in global clean energy. Likes the boundary between experimental and computational. 

6. EDI - *Joel*
"Research shows that teams with diverse backgrounds and perspectives solve complex engineering problems more effectively. Tell me about a time you worked on a technical project with someone whose background, training, or communication style was completely different from yours. How did you adapt your approach to make the collaboration successful?"

- First year uni design study proj. Group of 12 and become a company. Design a materials characterisation machine, hardness machine. Break everyone down into different roles. Very diverse team with different ethics. Frequent meetings with groups and sub groups. Made sure information was shared. This is excellent. Creat forums for communication. Listen to different perspectives. Made sure everyone was able to voice their opinion.
